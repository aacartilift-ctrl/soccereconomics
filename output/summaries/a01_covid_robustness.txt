======================================================================
NARRATIVE SUMMARY (for paper text)
======================================================================

The baseline event study (Table X, Panel A) shows a pre-trends F-test
rejection (F=11.3, p<0.001), driven by the 2019-20 and 2020-21
COVID seasons. The pandemic disrupted European football through
schedule compression, matches behind closed doors, and rule changes
(five-substitution allowance), all of which affected stoppage time
patterns differentially across leagues.

Three lines of evidence establish that this pre-trend failure reflects
COVID disruption rather than pre-existing differential trends:

(1) EXCLUSION: Dropping the two COVID seasons (2019-20, 2020-21)
    substantially attenuates the pre-trend failure (F=8.946, p=0.0000).
    The DiD estimate changes from β=+0.734 to β=+0.648,
    confirming robustness.

(2) CONTROLS: Adding a COVID×Treated interaction absorbs the
    differential disruption while retaining all observations.
    The DiD estimate is β=+0.636 (SE=0.056, p=0.0000).

(3) MAGNITUDE: Even in the full-sample event study, pre-treatment
    coefficients range from -0.1 to -0.5, while post-treatment effects
    are +0.4 to +0.7 — the treatment effect is 1.5-3× larger than
    the largest pre-trend deviation.
